# New Zealand

**New Zealand** (Māori: *Aotearoa*) is an island country in the southwestern Pacific Ocean. It consists of two main landmasses — the [[Geography of New Zealand|North Island]] (Te Ika-a-Māui) and the [[Geography of New Zealand|South Island]] (Te Waipounamu) — and over 600 smaller islands.[^1]

## Overview

New Zealand was the last large habitable land to be settled by humans. Between about 1280 and 1350, Polynesians began to settle the islands and subsequently developed a distinctive [[Māori Culture|Māori culture]]. See [[History of New Zealand]] for the full timeline.

> [!info] World Firsts
> New Zealand was the first nation to grant all women the right to vote (1893) and pioneered the adoption of compulsory arbitration between employers and unions (1894). It also guaranteed a minimum wage in 1894 — a world first.

A developed country, New Zealand ranks very highly in international measures of quality of life and human rights, and has one of the lowest levels of perceived corruption in the world. As of 2023, it is ranked second in the strength of its democratic institutions and third in government transparency.[^2]

The country's total land area of $268{,}000$ km² makes it the sixth-largest island country by area. Its exclusive economic zone is one of the world's largest, covering more than $15$ times its land area.
