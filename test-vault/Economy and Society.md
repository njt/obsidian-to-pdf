# Economy and Society

[[New Zealand]] is a developed country with a liberalised free-trade economy. During the 1980s, the country underwent major economic changes that transformed it from a protectionist to an open market economy.[^reforms]

## Economic Structure

The service sector dominates, followed by the industrial sector and agriculture. International tourism is also a significant source of revenue.

> [!tip] Key Relationship
> [[New Zealand]] and Australia have a strong Trans-Tasman relationship with free trade agreements and reciprocal travel arrangements allowing citizens to visit, live, and work in both countries without restrictions. As of 2013, approximately 650,000 New Zealand citizens (equivalent to $15\%$ of the resident population) lived in Australia.

## Demographics

The majority of New Zealand's population of around $5.3$ million is of European descent. The indigenous [[Māori Culture|Māori]] are the largest minority, followed by Asians and Pasifika peoples.

### Urbanisation

New Zealand is a highly urbanised country. Its largest cities by population:

1. **Auckland** — largest city, sunniest alongside Wellington and Christchurch (>2,000 sunshine hours/year)
2. **Wellington** — capital city, wettest major city (~1,200 mm rainfall/year)
3. **Christchurch** — driest major city (618 mm rainfall/year)

## International Relations

![[History of New Zealand#European Contact]]

New Zealand is a member of multiple international organisations. Key alliances:

- **ANZUS Treaty** (1951) — with Australia and the United States
- **Five Eyes** — intelligence alliance
- **Commonwealth of Nations** — as one of 15 Commonwealth realms
- **United Nations** — founding member

> [!warning] Nuclear-Free Policy
> New Zealand's nuclear-free policy led to the US suspending ANZUS obligations. Despite this, the treaty remained in effect between New Zealand and Australia. The policy was prompted in part by the sinking of the *Rainbow Warrior* by French agents in Auckland harbour in 1985.

Rugby union is closely linked to national identity. The **All Blacks** (the national rugby team) are famous for performing the haka before international matches.[^allblacks]
